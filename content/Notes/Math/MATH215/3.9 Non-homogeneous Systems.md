# Variation of Parameters

We can solve non-homogeneous system by using the variation of parameters method, consider the system:
$$
\vec{x'}(t) = A(t)\vec{x}  + \vec{f}(t)
$$
And suppose we solved the associated homogeneous equation $\vec{x'}=A \vec{x}$ and found the fundamental matrix solution $X(t)$. Similarly, we can use:
$$
\vec{x}(t) = \vec{x}_{p}(t) + \vec{x}_{c}(t)
$$
to construct our general solution. We can solve for $\vec{x}_{p}$ using the formula:
$$
\vec{x}_{p} = X(t) \int[X(t)]^{-1} \vec{f}(t)dt
$$

And the complementary solution has the form:

$$
\vec{x}_{c} = \vec{c}X(t)
$$
# Method of Undetermined Coefficients  
  
The method of undetermined coefficients is used to find a **particular solution** to a non-homogeneous linear system with **constant coefficients**:  
$$  
\vec{x}' = A\vec{x} + \vec{f}(t)  
$$  
where $A$ is a constant matrix.  
  
We write the general solution as  
$$  
\vec{x}(t) = \vec{x}_c(t) + \vec{x}_p(t)  
$$  
where:  
  
- $\vec{x}_c(t)$ solves the homogeneous system  
$$  
\vec{x}' = A\vec{x}  
$$  
  
- $\vec{x}_p(t)$ is a particular solution to  
$$  
\vec{x}' = A\vec{x} + \vec{f}(t)  
$$  
  
---  
  
## Idea of the Method  
  
We **guess** a form for $\vec{x}_p(t)$ based on the form of the forcing term $\vec{f}(t)$, with unknown constants, and then solve for those constants by substitution.  
  
---  
  
## Common Guesses  
  
### 1. Polynomial forcing  
If  
$$  
\vec{f}(t)  
$$  
is a polynomial of degree $n$, guess a vector polynomial of degree $n$:  
$$  
\vec{x}_p(t) = \vec{a}_n t^n + \vec{a}_{n-1} t^{n-1} + \cdots + \vec{a}_0  
$$  
  
---  
  
### 2. Exponential forcing  
If  
$$  
\vec{f}(t) = \vec{b}e^{\lambda t}  
$$  
guess  
$$  
\vec{x}_p(t) = \vec{c}e^{\lambda t}  
$$  
  
---  
  
### 3. Sine / cosine forcing  
If  
$$  
\vec{f}(t) = \vec{b}\cos(\omega t) + \vec{d}\sin(\omega t)  
$$  
guess  
$$  
\vec{x}_p(t) = \vec{c}\cos(\omega t) + \vec{e}\sin(\omega t)  
$$  
  
Even if the forcing only has cosine, it is usually safest to include both cosine and sine in the guess.  
  
---  
  
### 4. Exponential times polynomial / trig  
If the forcing is something like  
$$  
e^{\lambda t}p(t), \qquad e^{\lambda t}\cos(\omega t), \qquad e^{\lambda t}\sin(\omega t)  
$$  
then guess the same general type.  
  
For example, if  
$$  
\vec{f}(t) = \vec{b}e^{\lambda t}\cos(\omega t),  
$$  
guess  
$$  
\vec{x}_p(t) = e^{\lambda t}\big(\vec{c}\cos(\omega t) + \vec{d}\sin(\omega t)\big)  
$$  
  
---  
  
## Resonance Adjustment  
  
If your guess for $\vec{x}_p(t)$ overlaps with part of the complementary solution $\vec{x}_c(t)$, then the guess fails.  
  
In that case, multiply the whole guess by $t$ enough times until it becomes linearly independent from $\vec{x}_c(t)$.
