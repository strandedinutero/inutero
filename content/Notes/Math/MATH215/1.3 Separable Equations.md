# Separable Equations

When solving a simple ODE of the form $y' = f(x)$ we can simply integrate to obtain a general solution $y = \int f(x)dx + C$, but suppose if our ODE is a function of both $x$ and $y$ such that:

$$
y' = f(x,y)
$$
Then, we can say that it is *separable*, if it can be expressed as the following product:
$$
y' = f(x) \cdot g(y)
$$
Then, using the [[Inverse Function Theorem]], we can find its general solution as follows:

$$
\begin{align}
\frac{dy}{dx} & = f(x)\cdot g(y) \\ \\
\frac{dy}{g(y)} & = f(x)dx \\ \\
\int \frac{1}{g(y)}dy & = \int f(x)dx + C
\end{align}

$$

## Applications

Newton's law of cooling states that the rate at which the given temperature changes is proportional to the difference between the ambient temperature and the given temperature. Namely:

$$
\frac{dT}{dt} = k(A - T)
$$
Now, because this is separable we can solve for $T$ as a function of time:

$$
\begin{align}
\frac{1}{(T - A)} \cdot \frac{dT}{dt} & = -k \\
\int \frac{1}{T-A}dT & = \int -k dt   \\
\ln|T-A| & = -kt + C \\
T & = De^{ -kt } + A \quad\text{for some constant D =} e^{ C }



\end{align}
$$
