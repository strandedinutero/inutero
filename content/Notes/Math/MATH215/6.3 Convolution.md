# Laplace Transforms of Products

Convolution is what allows us to take the Laplace transform of a product, such that:
$$
(f\cdot g)(t):=\int^t_{0}f(\tau)g(t-\tau)d\tau
$$
Which is the convolution of $f$ and $g$.

Now, if $f$ and $g$ are of exponential order, then so is their convolution and we have that:

$$
\mathcal{L}\{(f\cdot g)(t)\}= \mathcal{L}\{\int^t_{0}f(\tau)g(t-\tau)d\tau\} = \mathcal{L}\{f(t)\}\mathcal{L}\{g(t)\}
$$

Similarly,
$$
\mathcal{L^{-1}}\{F(s)G(s)\} = \int^t_{0}f(\tau)g(t-\tau)d\tau
$$
## Solving ODEs With The Transfer Function and Convolution

Given the transfer function is defined as:
$$
H(s)=\frac{X(s)}{F(s)}
$$
We can solve for $x(t)$ using convolution with the transfer function and $F(s)$ such that:
$$
x(t)=\mathcal{L}^{-1}\{X(s)\}=\mathcal{L}^{-1}\{H(s)F(s)\}=\int^t_{0}\mathcal{L}^{-1}\{H(s)\}f(t-\tau)d\tau
$$
