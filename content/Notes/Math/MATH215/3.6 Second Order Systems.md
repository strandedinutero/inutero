# Second Order Systems

A second order system of equations can model a coupled mass-spring system and has the general form:
$$
M \vec{x''} = K \vec{x}
$$
Where:
$$

M = \begin{bmatrix}
m_{1} & 0 \\
0 & m_{2}
\end{bmatrix}
\quad \text{and} 
\quad 
K = \begin{bmatrix}
-(k_{1}+k_{2}) & k_{2} \\
 k_{2} & -(k_{2}+k_{3})

\end{bmatrix}
$$
But, we can divide by M, namely, we can multiply by its inverse to obtain:
$$
\vec{x''} = A \vec{x}
$$
Where
$$
A = M^{-1}K \quad \text{s.t.} \quad M^{-1} = \begin{bmatrix}
\frac{1}{m_{1}} & 0 \\
0 & \frac{1}{m_{2}}
\end{bmatrix}
$$
Then, the general solution is given by the formula:

$$
\vec{x}(t) = \sum_{i=1}^n \vec{v_{i}}(a_{i}\cos(\omega_{i}t))+b_{i}\sin(\omega_{i}t))
$$

Where $-\omega_{i}^2 = \lambda_{i}$ and $a_i$ and $b_i$ are constants.

This formula applies to an undamped, unforced coupled mass-spring system with positive masses and spring constants.

## Forced Oscillations

For forced oscillations, the second order system has the form:
$$
\vec{x''}=A \vec{x} + \vec{F}\cos(\omega t) \quad A=M^{-1}K
$$
To find a general solution we again need to find a particular solution such that:

$$
\vec{x}(t) = \vec{x_{c}(t)} + \vec{x_{p}}(t)
$$
For this, we can guess
$$
\vec{x}_p(t)=\vec{c}\cos(\omega t)+\vec{d}\sin(\omega t)
$$
## Resonance Condition

If $A+\omega^2I$ is invertible, then $\vec{d}=0$ and
$$
\vec{c}=-(A+\omega^2I)^{-1}\vec{F}.
$$

Thus,
$$
\vec{x}_p(t)=-(A+\omega^2I)^{-1}\vec{F}\cos(\omega t).
$$

If $A+\omega^2I$ is ==not== invertible, then $-\omega^2$ is an eigenvalue of $A$, meaning that $\omega$ ==is a natural frequency of the system==. In this case resonance occurs, and the usual trial solution fails. One must instead try a solution of the form
$$
\vec{x}_p(t)=t\big(\vec{c}\cos(\omega t)+\vec{d}\sin(\omega t)\big).
$$

The complementary solution $\vec{x}_c(t)$ represents the transient motion, while the particular solution $\vec{x}_p(t)$ represents the forced response.
