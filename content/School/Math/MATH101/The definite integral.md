
The definite integral,
$$
\int_a^b f(x)
$$
represents the *net signed area* under the curve of $f(x)$ from $a\to b$. 

This area can be approximated numerically via Riemann sums, and other methods that will be touched on later.

The Riemann sum seeks to approximate the area by taking the sum of many many many rectangles, and when we take the limit of these rectangles at infinity we land on the exact value of the definite integral above.
